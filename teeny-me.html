Teeny Me

A creative dress-up game where you design your avatar, style outfits, and build your dream space

The Concept

Teeny Me is a mobile dress-up game that brings back the joy of childhood creativity. It started as a school UI design project, but grew into something I'm passionate about turning into a real game.

I drew inspiration from the dress-up games I loved as a kid—sites like Dressupgames.com and Doll Divine. As an artist, I wanted to create something authentic by drawing all the game assets myself, giving it a personal, hand-crafted feel.

Every asset in the game is hand-drawn by me, from the clothing pieces to the room furniture. That personal touch is what makes Teeny Me feel different from other dress-up games.

I've built a rough playable demo that shows the core mechanics, and I'm excited about the potential to develop this further into a full release.

The goal is to create a space where players feel genuinely seen — where the game reflects their personality back at them through every outfit choice and room they design.

Key Features

Create Your Avatar

Design a unique character with customizable features, expressions, and accessories

Mix & Match Outfits

Endless clothing combinations to express your personal style

Design Your Room

Decorate and customize your avatar's living space with furniture and decor

Take Care of Your Avatar

Feed, play with, and nurture your character to keep them happy

The Vision

Teeny Me started as a class project, but it's grown into something I'm passionate about developing into a full mobile game. The positive feedback on the concept and art style has motivated me to continue building it out.

I'm currently working on expanding the asset library, refining the gameplay mechanics, and improving the overall user experience. My goal is to create a game that captures the creativity and joy of classic dress-up games while bringing something fresh and personal to the genre.

Game Preview

Teeny Me is a character-driven dress-up experience centred on creativity, personalization, and social interaction. Players begin by designing a fully customizable avatar, with control over facial features, hairstyles, and styling details to create a character that reflects their identity. From there, they can explore a wide range of clothing options, mixing and matching outfits to experiment with different aesthetics and fashion styles.

Beyond styling, the experience encourages a sense of care and connection through interactive features such as feeding and tending to the avatar, making it feel more like a companion than a static character. Players can also design and customize their avatar’s living space, arranging furniture and decor to create a personalized environment that reflects their taste.

To extend the experience socially, Teeny Me includes an in-app sharing system where players can upload their styled outfits, browse others’ creations, and engage through likes and comments. This feature fosters a sense of community and allows players to showcase their creativity while drawing inspiration from others.

Overall, Teeny Me combines fashion, customization, and social features to create an engaging and expressive digital space where players can explore identity, creativity, and interaction.

Design Process

Concept Art

Early character explorations focused on developing a cohesive visual style while experimenting with different expressions, proportions, and outfit combinations. Each character was hand-drawn to convey a distinct personality and aesthetic, helping establish the tone and direction of the game. These initial concepts also informed how customization options could translate into the final in-game avatar system.

Wireframes & Layout Design

(Please look from left to right)

These low-fidelity wireframes explore the app’s overall structure, information architecture, and core user flow. Starting from the main entry point, the layouts map how users navigate between key features such as avatar customization, outfit selection, and in-game interactions like feeding and room design.

The focus at this stage was on clarity and usability—ensuring that menus, categories, and controls are intuitive and easy to access. By simplifying the visuals, the wireframes allowed for quick iteration on layout decisions and helped establish a strong foundation before moving into higher-fidelity designs.
